St. Mark Cathedral

Address Infanta, 4336 Quezon

Infanta, Quezon, is a coastal town in the Philippines known for its rich history and serene atmosphere. Nestled between the Sierra Madre mountains and the Pacific Ocean, it offers a unique blend of natural beauty and historical significance.

One of the town's most notable landmarks is the Church of Saint Mark the Evangelist, which was established in 1578. Initially constructed from bamboo and nipa, the church was rebuilt with stone in 1732 after a fire destroyed the original structure. Over the years, it has stood as a testament to the town's resilience and devotion. The church also played a vital role in the community, housing a tribunal and serving as a center for primary education, funded by the local people.
